## DAY 1 — FOUNDATIONS OF MSS INSTRUCTION

### Block 1 — Adult Learning Principles
**Hours:** 1.5 | **Method:** Seminar | **Time:** 0830–1000

**Purpose:** MSS trainees are adults with operational experience, existing mental models, and immediate performance needs. The instructional approach must match how adults actually learn technical skills.

**TLO:** Given the six principles of adult learning (Knowles), the candidate will explain how each principle applies to MSS instruction and identify at least one instructional technique for each.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Cover Knowles' six principles: Need to Know, Self-Concept, Experience, Readiness, Orientation, Motivation
- Kolb experiential learning cycle: Concrete Experience → Reflective Observation → Abstract Conceptualization → Active Experimentation
- Scaffolding: progressive removal of support structures. Demo the concept using the TM-20 pipeline building progression (follow-along → guided → independent → assessed)
- Ask candidates to share their worst experience as a student in a military course. Connect their frustrations to violated adult learning principles.

**Student Activity:** In pairs, analyze a provided TM-20 lesson plan block. Identify where each of Knowles' six principles is or is not addressed. Present findings to group (5 minutes per pair).

**Assessment:** Formative — instructor observes discussion quality and principle identification accuracy.

---

### Block 2 — Army Instructional Methodology
**Hours:** 1.75 | **Method:** Seminar | **Time:** 1015–1200

**Purpose:** MSS instruction operates within an Army regulatory framework. Candidates must understand how TLOs/ELOs, TCS, and methods of instruction create the structure that ensures consistency across all instructors and iterations.

**TLO:** The candidate will construct a TCS (Task-Condition-Standard) statement from a provided learning requirement and correctly identify the appropriate method of instruction for a given content type.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- TCS framework: Task (action verb), Condition (circumstances), Standard (measurable criteria). Use a TM-20 pipeline building task as the worked example.
- TLO vs. ELO hierarchy: TLO = what the trainee can do at lesson end; ELOs = sub-skills that build to TLO
- Methods of instruction: LEC, DIS, SEM, LAB, WKS, BRF, EVAL — when each is appropriate. Emphasize: MSS courses are 60-70% LAB.

**Student Activity:** Each candidate writes 1 TCS statement for a TM-10 task of their choice. Group reviews and critiques — does the standard pass the "two evaluators would agree" test?

**Assessment:** Formative — TCS quality reviewed by group.

---

### Block 3 — MSS Training Philosophy
**Hours:** 1.5 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1300–1430

**Purpose:** MSS instruction is lab-dominant, operationally framed, and evaluation-certified. This block establishes the concrete instructional patterns candidates will use.

**TLO:** The candidate will demonstrate the demonstration-practice-feedback loop and explain the 75% rule for lab time allocation.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- The 75% rule: in any lab block, trainees should have hands on keyboard for at least 75% of the time
- Demonstration-practice-feedback loop: Demonstrate (5 min) → Practice (15 min) → Feedback (5 min) → Repeat
- Exercise design walkthrough: show how a TM-20 lab exercise is structured to follow this loop
- Contrast effective vs. ineffective instruction: play a 3-minute video clip of an instructor lecturing over a lab exercise (ineffective) vs. instructor demonstrating then releasing to practice (effective)

**Student Activity:** Each candidate maps a TM-10 or TM-20 lesson block to the demo-practice-feedback loop. Identify the demonstrate, practice, and feedback moments. Identify any block where the loop is incomplete.

**Assessment:** Formative — instructor reviews mapping accuracy.

### Block 4 — Pre-Course Checklist Execution
**Hours:** 1.25 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1445–1600

**Purpose:** A failed pre-course setup wastes the first hours of Day 1 — hours that cannot be recovered.

**TLO:** Given the universal pre-course checklist, the candidate will execute all steps for a simulated TM-20 course setup, identifying and resolving 2 deliberately planted setup issues.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Walk through the universal pre-course checklist from the Concepts Guide §5-2
- Environment verification: test logins, verify access levels, confirm training data currency
- Account provisioning coordination: who to contact, timelines, backup plans
- 2 planted issues: one student account at wrong access level, one workstation with wrong environment URL bookmarked

**Student Activity:** Execute the pre-course checklist on a simulated 6-student TM-20 class roster. Discover and resolve both planted issues.

**Assessment:** Formative — both issues must be identified and resolved.

### Block 5 — Instructor Performance Standards
**Hours:** 1.0 | **Method:** Seminar | **Time:** 1600–1700

**Purpose:** Candidates must understand how they will be evaluated — both in the Day 5 microteaching and in every future observation.

**TLO:** The candidate will explain the 7 observation criteria and identify the 2 hard No-Go criteria for instructor evaluation.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Walk through each criterion from FDP §6-1: Technical Accuracy, Instructional Clarity, Student Engagement, Check on Learning, Lab Management, Evaluation Fidelity, Course Materials Currency
- Hard No-Go criteria: Technical Accuracy, Evaluation Fidelity
- Show a completed Instructor Observation Report (FDP Appendix B) — discuss what satisfactory and unsatisfactory look like
- Preview: "These 7 criteria are what you will be scored on during microteaching on Day 5."

**Student Activity:** Review a sample observation report with one unsatisfactory criterion. Discuss: what went wrong? What should the instructor do differently?

**Assessment:** Formative.

## DAY 2 — PLATFORM DEEP-DIVE FOR INSTRUCTION

### Block 6 — TM-10 Instructor Walkthrough
**Hours:** 2.0 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 0830–1030

**Purpose:** Every TM-10 lab step from the instructor seat — expected outcomes and failure modes.

**TLO:** The candidate will execute every TM-10 lab exercise from the instructor account, identifying the expected outcome and at least one common failure mode for each step.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Walk through every TM-10 lab exercise sequentially from the instructor MSS account
- For each step: what the trainee should see, what goes wrong most often, how to diagnose, how to resolve
- Focus on Block 2 (navigation — where most students get lost) and Block 6 (classification — highest stakes)
- Demonstrate the instructor's "second screen" technique: instructor shows their screen while trainees follow on theirs

**Student Activity:** Candidates execute TM-10 labs in parallel with the walkthrough. Each candidate documents 1 failure mode per block that they identify independently.

**Assessment:** Formative — failure mode documentation reviewed.

---

### Block 7 — TM-20 Instructor Walkthrough
**Hours:** 1.25 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1045–1200

**Purpose:** TM-20 has the highest failure rate at the pipeline building stage. Instructors must know every failure mode.

**TLO:** The candidate will execute the TM-20 pipeline building, Workshop, and governance exercises from the instructor seat, diagnosing at least 2 pipeline build failures.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Focus on pipeline build failures: type mismatch on join, wrong join type (inner vs. left), missing column after schema change
- Workshop: layout issues (widget overlap, filter on wrong data source)
- Governance: metadata omission is the most common No-Go reason on TM-20 — the instructor must check governance before the trainee submits

**Student Activity:** Candidates execute TM-20 pipeline lab. Instructor injects 2 pipeline failures during the exercise; candidates must diagnose without assistance.

**Assessment:** Formative — both failures must be diagnosed.

---

### Block 8 — TM-30 Instructor Walkthrough
**Hours:** 2.0 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1300–1500

**Purpose:** TM-30 is design-centric. The instructor must facilitate design critiques, not just troubleshoot technical errors.

**TLO:** The candidate will walk through the TM-30 Ontology design critique exercise and AIP Logic configuration lab from the instructor seat, demonstrating the design critique facilitation approach.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Ontology design critique: how to facilitate without designing for the trainee; the 6-item rubric as the arbiter
- AIP Logic configuration: common errors (wrong trigger type, input/output mismatch)
- Quiver linked views: the most common No-Go on TM-30; ensure candidates understand the link direction concept

**Student Activity:** Candidates review a deliberately flawed Ontology design and provide critique using the 6-item rubric. Compare critiques across the group.

**Assessment:** Formative — rubric application accuracy reviewed.

---

### Block 9 — Environment Failure Diagnosis
**Hours:** 1.25 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1515–1630

**Purpose:** Instructors must resolve environment issues without escalation. These 6 failures cover 90%+ of issues encountered during course delivery.

**TLO:** The candidate will diagnose and resolve 6 pre-staged environment failures within prescribed time limits without external assistance.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- 6 pre-staged failures: CAC auth, expired account, wrong environment, missing project access, stale data, pipeline schema change
- Each has a time limit (2-10 minutes). The candidate must diagnose root cause and resolve — not just workaround.
- Debrief each failure after the exercise: root cause, resolution, prevention

**Student Activity:** Candidates rotate through 6 stations, each with a pre-staged failure. Resolve each within the time limit.

**Assessment:** Formative — all 6 failures must be resolved. Failures not resolved in time are debriefed and re-attempted.

### Block 10 — T&EO Scoring Calibration
**Hours:** 0.5 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1630–1700

**Purpose:** Two evaluators watching the same performance must reach the same Go/No-Go decision. Calibration is the process of getting there.

**TLO:** Given 2 recorded trainee performances, the candidate will score T&EOs independently and reach consensus with other candidates on the Go/No-Go decision.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Play 2 pre-recorded trainee performances (one clear Go, one borderline)
- Each candidate scores independently using the T&EO scoring sheet
- Group comparison: identify discrepancies, discuss, reach consensus
- The borderline case is the teaching moment — this is where evaluator drift occurs

**Student Activity:** Score both performances. Participate in calibration discussion. Document the reasoning for each decision.

**Assessment:** Formative — consensus must be reached on both cases.

## DAY 3 — LAB FACILITATION AND ERROR MANAGEMENT

### Block 11 — Facilitation Techniques
**Hours:** 1.5 | **Method:** Seminar | **Time:** 0830–1000

**Purpose:** Lab facilitation is where most instruction actually happens. The instructor's ability to circulate, unblock, and manage time determines whether trainees learn.

**TLO:** The candidate will describe the circulation pattern, the help gradient, and 3 dead time elimination techniques.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Circulation: walk the whole room first, assist the most stuck student, don't linger, second pass to verify
- Help gradient: "Read the error" → "What step are you on?" → "Walk through the thinking" → never take the keyboard during labs
- Time management: announce time checks, 50% rule for group catch-up, cap individual assistance at 3 minutes
- Dead time elimination: discussion questions during builds, stretch tasks for early finishers, peer pairing

**Student Activity:** Discuss: "What is the worst dead time you've experienced in a training course? What caused it? How could the instructor have prevented it?"

**Assessment:** Formative.

---

### Block 12 — Simulated Lab Facilitation Rotation
**Hours:** 1.25 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1015–1130

**Purpose:** Practice facilitating under realistic conditions with heterogeneous "trainees."

**TLO:** The candidate will facilitate a 15-minute lab segment with role-players of varying skill levels and receive peer/instructor feedback on facilitation technique.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Each candidate facilitates a 15-minute segment of a TM-10 or TM-20 lab
- Other candidates role-play trainees: one advanced (finishes early, asks stretch questions), one struggling (quiet, stuck, won't ask for help), one average
- Instructor observes circulation, help gradient, time management, engagement of the struggling role-player

**Student Activity:** Facilitate the assigned lab segment. Receive feedback from peers and instructor.

**Assessment:** Formative — feedback on all facilitation criteria.

### Block 13 — Common Trainee Error Taxonomy
**Hours:** 0.5 | **Method:** Seminar | **Time:** 1130–1200

**Purpose:** Cataloging common errors allows faster diagnosis and better prevention.

**TLO:** The candidate will identify the top 10 common trainee errors by course level and the documented intervention for each.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Walk through the top 10 error taxonomy from the Concepts Guide §4-2
- For each: error, root cause, intervention
- Emphasize: many errors are preventable by briefing the error before the step where it occurs

**Student Activity:** Review the taxonomy. Each candidate identifies 1 additional error they have personally observed (or anticipate) not in the taxonomy.

**Assessment:** Formative.

---

### Block 14 — Error Management Practice
**Hours:** 1.5 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1300–1430

**Purpose:** Apply the error taxonomy under pressure — diagnose and intervene in real time during a simulated lab.

**TLO:** The candidate will correctly diagnose and intervene on at least 4 of 5 simulated trainee errors using the documented interventions.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Mock lab session with role-players making errors from the taxonomy
- 5 errors injected over 90 minutes — candidates must identify, diagnose, and intervene
- Debrief each error: was the diagnosis correct? Was the intervention appropriate? Was the error preventable?

**Student Activity:** Facilitate the mock lab. Diagnose and intervene on errors as they arise.

**Assessment:** Formative — 4 of 5 correct diagnosis and intervention required.

### Block 15 — Coaching vs. Evaluating
**Hours:** 1.25 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1445–1600

**Purpose:** The instructor-evaluator dual role is the most difficult aspect of MSS instruction. The assistance trap is the most common evaluator failure.

**TLO:** The candidate will demonstrate a clear mode transition from coaching to evaluating, maintain evaluator discipline (no assistance) during a simulated evaluation, and identify the assistance trap.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- The coaching mode: help, guide, scaffold, unblock
- The evaluating mode: observe, score, document — no assistance of any kind
- Mode transition: announce clearly to the class
- The assistance trap: wanting the trainee to succeed so badly that you help during the evaluation, voiding the result
- Practice: role-play an evaluation where the "trainee" is struggling and asking for help. The candidate must maintain evaluator discipline.

**Student Activity:** Each candidate role-plays a 5-minute evaluation where the role-player seeks assistance. Candidate must maintain evaluator mode.

**Assessment:** Formative — instructor observes whether candidate maintains evaluator discipline.

---

### Block 16 — Check-on-Learning Design
**Hours:** 1.0 | **Method:** Seminar | **Time:** 1600–1700

**Purpose:** Effective check-on-learning questions verify comprehension at multiple levels and provide real-time feedback on whether the class is ready to move forward.

**TLO:** The candidate will write a 3-question check-on-learning set for a provided lesson block at recall, application, and analysis levels.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Bloom's taxonomy (simplified): Recall → Application → Analysis
- Recall: "What data type must both columns be for a join?" (single correct answer)
- Application: "Your pipeline output shows 0 rows after a join. What do you check first?" (apply diagnostic procedure)
- Analysis: "A trainee's pipeline produces 3x the expected rows after a join. What went wrong and why?" (analyze root cause)
- Avoid yes/no questions — they don't verify comprehension

**Student Activity:** Each candidate writes a 3-question set for a TM-20 lesson block. Group reviews: does each question test the intended level?

**Assessment:** Formative — question set quality reviewed.

## DAY 4 — ASSESSMENT DESIGN AND EVALUATION STANDARDIZATION

### Block 17 — T&EO Structure Deep-Dive
**Hours:** 1.5 | **Method:** Seminar | **Time:** 0830–1000

**Purpose:** T&EOs are the foundation of all MSS evaluations. Candidates must understand every component.

**TLO:** The candidate will explain each T&EO component (task number, title, condition, standard, performance measures, critical items) and describe how a T&EO is constructed from a TCS objective.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Walk through a complete T&EO (TM20-03 recommended — pipeline building)
- Trace from TCS → TLO → T&EO: show how the standard becomes performance measures
- Critical items: what makes an item critical (safety, security, operational consequence of failure)
- Common T&EO design errors: standard too vague ("build a pipeline"), too specific ("click the blue button on the left")

**Student Activity:** Deconstruct TM20-03: identify the TCS origin, trace to performance measures, identify critical items.

**Assessment:** Formative.

---

### Block 18 — Assessment Design Practicum
**Hours:** 1.75 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1015–1200

**Purpose:** Candidates practice writing performance measures — the hardest part of assessment design.

**TLO:** The candidate will draft 3 performance measures for a provided TLO that meet T&EO quality standards (observable, measurable, traceable to standard).

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Provide a TLO (e.g., "Configure a scheduled pipeline with email alerting on build failure")
- Each candidate drafts 3 performance measures independently
- Peer review: are the measures observable? Measurable? Would two evaluators score them the same way?
- Group refinement: iterate until all 3 measures meet quality standards

**Student Activity:** Draft 3 performance measures. Peer review. Refine.

**Assessment:** Formative — final measures reviewed by instructor for quality.

---

### Block 19 — Evaluation Calibration (Extended)
**Hours:** 1.5 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1300–1430

**Purpose:** Full calibration exercise with 3 recorded performances of varying quality.

**TLO:** The candidate will score 3 recorded trainee performances independently and reach group consensus on all 3 Go/No-Go decisions.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Performance 1: Clear Go (all measures met cleanly)
- Performance 2: Clear No-Go (critical item failed)
- Performance 3: Borderline (non-critical items failed; overall determination depends on count)
- Each candidate scores all 3 independently before group discussion
- Focus on Performance 3: this is where evaluator drift occurs; calibrate the group to a common standard

**Student Activity:** Score all 3 performances. Group calibration discussion. Document consensus rationale.

**Assessment:** Formative — consensus must be reached on all 3; individual scoring documented.

---

### Block 20 — Exam Administration Procedures
**Hours:** 1.25 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1445–1600

**Purpose:** Pre-test and post-test administration follows specific procedures. Errors in administration compromise the diagnostic (pre-test) or evaluative (post-test) value.

**TLO:** The candidate will administer a mock pre-test following standard procedures: timing, answer key security, scoring, and documentation.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Pre-test: diagnostic only, Day 1 first 20 minutes; does not count toward Go/No-Go
- Post-test: evaluative, final day; passing score per POI (typically 70-80%)
- Answer key handling: instructor-only document; never distributed; print student versions without answers
- Scoring: use the answer key exactly; do not give partial credit unless the answer key specifies it
- Documentation: record individual scores; compute class mean; report in course completion package

**Student Activity:** Administer a mock pre-test to role-players. Score using the answer key. Compute class mean.

**Assessment:** Formative — procedure compliance reviewed.

---

### Block 21 — No-Go Counseling
**Hours:** 1.0 | **Method:** Lab | **Time:** 1600–1700

**Purpose:** Delivering a No-Go result is an instructor responsibility that must be immediate, specific, documented, and constructive.

**TLO:** The candidate will deliver a simulated No-Go counseling session that identifies specific deficiencies, documents the result, and proposes a remediation plan.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- No-Go counseling is same-day, private, face-to-face
- Structure: "Here is what you were evaluated on. Here is where you did not meet the standard. Here is what you need to do to pass."
- Document: specific performance measures not met, why, remediation actions, timeline
- Tone: constructive, not punitive. The goal is remediation.
- Role-play: candidate delivers a No-Go to a role-player who is disappointed/frustrated. Maintain professionalism.

**Student Activity:** Each candidate delivers a simulated No-Go counseling. Peer and instructor feedback.

**Assessment:** Formative — instructor observes specificity, documentation, and tone.

## DAY 5 — MICROTEACHING AND EVALUATION

### Block 22 — Microteaching (Evaluated)
**Hours:** 3.5 | **Method:** Evaluation | **Time:** 0830–1200

**Purpose:** The microteaching block is the primary assessed performance for T3-I Phase 1.

**TLO:** The candidate will deliver a 20-minute block of instruction demonstrating satisfactory performance on at least 5 of 7 instructor observation criteria, with no unsatisfactory on Technical Accuracy or Evaluation Fidelity.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- Each candidate delivers a 20-minute block selected from TM-10, TM-20, or TM-30
- Evaluator (Senior or Master Instructor) scores all 7 criteria
- Classmates serve as role-play trainees at the appropriate course level
- After each microteaching: 5-minute peer feedback, then next candidate
- Schedule 25 minutes per candidate (20 delivery + 5 feedback); 8 candidates = 3.3 hours

**Student Activity:** Deliver the prepared 20-minute block. Receive peer feedback.

**Assessment:** **EVALUATED.** Go: 5/7 satisfactory with no hard No-Go. No-Go: < 5/7 satisfactory, or unsatisfactory on Technical Accuracy or Evaluation Fidelity.

---

### Block 23 — Written Exam (Evaluated)
**Hours:** 1.0 | **Method:** Evaluation | **Time:** 1300–1400

**Purpose:** Validates knowledge of instructional methodology, T&EO structure, evaluation procedures, and instructor performance standards.

**TLO:** The candidate will achieve ≥ 80% (40/50) on the written exam.

**Key Delivery Notes:**
- 16 questions (50 points): mix of multiple choice and short answer
- Topics: adult learning principles, TCS framework, T&EO structure, Go/No-Go procedures, evaluation calibration, error management, instructor performance standards, pre-course administration
- Closed book, closed notes
- 60 minutes

**Student Activity:** Complete the written exam.

**Assessment:** **EVALUATED.** Go: ≥ 80%. No-Go: < 80%. One re-take authorized within 10 duty days.

**Key Delivery Notes:**

- Go/No-Go results: announced individually, not publicly
