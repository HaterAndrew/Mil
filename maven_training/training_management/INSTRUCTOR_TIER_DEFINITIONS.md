## SECTION 1 — PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1-1. Purpose

This document defines the instructor tier hierarchy for the MSS Training Program. It formalizes the progression from initial certification through Master Instructor, establishing the criteria, authorities, and responsibilities for each tier. It replaces the previous flat instructor model where all certified instructors held equivalent authority.

### 1-2. Scope

This document applies to:
- All C2DAO certified instructors
- All Unit Data Trainers (T3-F graduates)
- C2DAO Training OIC and designated supervisors
- Commanders nominating personnel for instructor roles

## SECTION 2 — TIER STRUCTURE

### 2-1. Overview

The MSS Training Program uses a four-tier instructor hierarchy. The first three tiers (Instructor, Senior Instructor, Master Instructor) apply to C2DAO-certified instructors who deliver the full TM-10 through TM-50 curriculum. The fourth tier (Unit Data Trainer) is a separate designation for MSC-level personnel who sustain TM-10 delivery and TM-20 refresher facilitation locally.

```
                    ┌─────────────────────┐
                    │  Master Instructor   │  ← Certifies Seniors; curriculum authority
                    └──────────┬──────────┘
                               │
                    ┌──────────┴──────────┐
                    │  Senior Instructor   │  ← Certifies Instructors; lesson plan authority
                    └──────────┬──────────┘
                               │
                    ┌──────────┴──────────┐
                    │     Instructor       │  ← Delivers courses; conducts Go/No-Go
                    └─────────────────────┘


        ┌─────────────────────────┐
        │   Unit Data Trainer     │  ← TM-10 delivery + TM-20 refresher (MSC-level)
        └─────────────────────────┘
```

### 2-2. Tier Summary

| Tier | Prerequisite | Authority | Designated By |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instructor | T3-I Go + domain quals per FDP §2-1 | Deliver assigned courses; conduct Go/No-Go evaluations for courses up to one level below own certification | C2DAO Training OIC |
| Senior Instructor | Instructor + 12 months + 4 iterations + satisfactory annual observation + OIC recommendation | Observe and certify new instructors (Phases 1-4); approve lesson plan revisions; serve as evaluator for any course up to own tier | C2DAO Training OIC |
| Master Instructor | Senior + 24 months as Senior + 2 cross-certifications + mentored 2 instructors to certification + OIC designation | Certify Senior Instructors; approve curriculum changes; serve as certification authority delegate when Training OIC is unavailable; evaluate instructor candidates for any course | C2DAO Training OIC |
| Unit Data Trainer | T3-F Go + unit CDR nomination | Deliver TM-10 independently; facilitate TM-20 refresher labs (Blocks 1-10); administer TM-10 Go/No-Go evaluations; proctor TM-10 pre/post exams as standalone activity | C2DAO Training OIC (upon T3-F completion) |

## SECTION 3 — TIER CRITERIA (DETAILED)

### 3-1. Instructor

**Prerequisites:**
- T3-I Phase 1 Go (classroom portion — 5 days)
- T3-I Phase 2 complete (co-teach + lead observed — scheduled separately)
- Domain qualification per FDP §2-1 for the course(s) to be taught
- Satisfactory Instructor Observation Report from Phase 2 lead observed iteration

**Authorities:**
- Deliver assigned course(s) independently
- Conduct Go/No-Go evaluations for courses at or below own certification level
- Submit lesson improvement proposals to Senior Instructor or Training OIC
- Participate in semi-annual instructor PD sessions

**Limitations:**
- Cannot observe or certify other instructors
- Cannot approve lesson plan revisions (submit proposals only)
- Cannot modify evaluation criteria or T&EOs
- Cannot approve enrollment exceptions

**Sustainment:**
- All requirements in FDP §4 apply (platform currency, annual observation, PD sessions)
- Must teach at least 1 course iteration per 12 months to maintain certification

### 3-2. Senior Instructor

**Prerequisites:**
- Active Instructor certification for at least 12 months
- Minimum 4 complete course iterations delivered as lead instructor
- Satisfactory annual observation for most recent observation period
- C2DAO Training OIC written recommendation
- Demonstrated ability to mentor — must have served as supervising instructor for at least 1 Phase 2 candidate

**Authorities:**
- All Instructor authorities, plus:
- Observe instructor candidates during Phases 1-3 and provide formal observation reports
- Certify new Instructors upon satisfactory completion of Phase 3 (when designated by Training OIC)
- Approve lesson plan revisions for courses within own certification scope
- Serve as evaluator for any course up to own certification level
- Serve as designated observer for annual instructor observations
- Conduct evaluator certification observations

**Limitations:**
- Cannot certify Senior or Master Instructors
- Cannot approve curriculum-level changes (new courses, course restructuring, T&EO modifications)
- Cannot serve as certification authority delegate without Master Instructor designation

**Promotion Process:**
1. Instructor self-nominates or is nominated by Training OIC
2. Training OIC reviews service record: teaching iterations, observation reports, mentoring history, platform currency
3. Training OIC issues written recommendation or documents deficiencies
4. Upon approval, Senior Instructor designation is entered in the Instructor Roster

### 3-3. Master Instructor

**Prerequisites:**
- Active Senior Instructor for at least 24 months
- Minimum 2 cross-certifications (certified to teach at least 3 distinct courses)
- Has mentored at least 2 instructor candidates from Phase 1 through Phase 4 certification
- C2DAO Training OIC designation (written memorandum)
- Demonstrated curriculum development capability — has authored or substantially revised at least 1 lesson plan

**Authorities:**
- All Senior Instructor authorities, plus:
- Certify Senior Instructors
- Approve curriculum changes (new lessons, course restructuring, T&EO modifications) within scope of own certification
- Serve as certification authority delegate when the C2DAO Training OIC is unavailable (e.g., TDY, PCS transition, vacancy)
- Evaluate instructor candidates for any course regardless of own certification (evaluating instructional technique, not domain content)
- Represent C2DAO Training Division in external program reviews and accreditation discussions

**Limitations:**
- Cannot certify other Master Instructors (reserved for Training OIC)
- Cannot unilaterally change prerequisite requirements (requires Training OIC and POI update)
- Delegation as certification authority is temporary and reverts when Training OIC returns

**Designation Process:**
1. Training OIC identifies need for Master Instructor (succession planning, program growth, geographic distribution)
2. Training OIC reviews candidate record against all prerequisites
3. Training OIC issues designation memorandum
4. Master Instructor designation is entered in the Instructor Roster with effective date

### 3-4. Unit Data Trainer

**Prerequisites:**
- T3-F Go (3-day MSC Force Multiplier course)
- TM-20 Go on file (minimum)
- Unit commander nomination letter on file with C2DAO
- Assignment to a billet or duty position with training responsibilities (data NCO, S6 staff, unit data steward, or equivalent)

**Authorities:**
- Deliver TM-10 (Maven User) independently at unit level
- Facilitate TM-20 refresher lab sessions (Blocks 1-10 only: project creation, file ingestion, pipeline basics)
- Administer TM-10 Go/No-Go evaluations using T&EO scoring sheets
- Proctor TM-10 pre-test and post-test as standalone activity for self-study or remote personnel
- Report training completion status to C2DAO via Unit Training Status Report

**Limitations:**
- Cannot deliver TM-20 initial certification (requires C2DAO-certified instructor)
- Cannot deliver TM-30 or any TM-40/TM-50 course
- Cannot modify curriculum, lesson plans, or evaluation criteria
- Cannot grant enrollment exceptions or waive prerequisites
- Cannot certify other instructors or Unit Data Trainers
- Cannot issue instructor certifications of any kind

**Sustainment:**
- Deliver at least 1 TM-10 iteration per quarter
- Attend semi-annual C2DAO PD session (virtual attendance authorized for geographically separated UDTs)
- Annual re-certification by C2DAO observation (virtual observation authorized)
- Designation lapses after 6 months without delivering a TM-10 iteration; reinstatement requires re-observation by C2DAO

**Reporting:**
- Submit Unit Training Status Report to C2DAO within 5 duty days of each TM-10 iteration
- Report No-Go results to C2DAO within 24 hours (No-Go counseling and remediation scheduling remain the unit's responsibility)
- Report environment or access issues that exceed UDT troubleshooting capability to C2DAO within 48 hours

## SECTION 4 — TRANSITION FROM CURRENT MODEL

### 4-1. Grandfathering Existing Instructors

Instructors certified under the previous 4-phase apprenticeship model (FDP §3-1, pre-T3-I) are recognized as follows:

| Current Status | Transition Tier | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 4 certified, < 12 months teaching | Instructor | No additional requirement |
| Phase 4 certified, 12+ months teaching, 4+ iterations | Senior Instructor (eligible) | Training OIC reviews record and issues recommendation within 60 days of this document's effective date |
| Training OIC or acting Training OIC | Master Instructor (eligible) | Commander confirms designation within 60 days |

Grandfathered instructors are not required to complete T3-I Phase 1 (classroom). They are required to attend the next scheduled semi-annual PD session, which will include a T3-I orientation block covering the tier structure, SME designation rubric, and updated observation criteria.
