## SECTION 1 — PURPOSE

### 1-1. Purpose

This guide establishes succession planning procedures for the MSS Training Program's key positions. The C2DAO Training OIC is the single certification authority for all MSS instructors and the final approver for curriculum changes. Without a documented succession plan, this position represents a single point of failure for the entire training program.

### 1-2. Key Positions

| Position | Current Authority | Risk if Vacant |
|---|---|---|
| C2DAO Training OIC | All instructor certifications, curriculum approval, SME designations, T&EO authority, program representation | Training program stops certifying instructors; no new courses approved; no SME designations issued |
| Senior Instructor(s) | Instructor certification (when designated), lesson plan approval, evaluator certification | New instructors cannot complete Phase 3-4; lesson plans stagnate |
| Master Instructor(s) | Senior Instructor certification, curriculum changes, delegation as certification authority | No backup for Training OIC; no Senior Instructor promotions |

## SECTION 2 — SUCCESSION FOR C2DAO TRAINING OIC

### 2-1. Delegation of Authority

When the C2DAO Training OIC is unavailable (TDY, leave, PCS transition, medical, vacancy):

| Duration | Delegated To | Delegated Authorities |
|---|---|---|
| < 15 duty days | Designated Master Instructor | All Training OIC authorities except: creating new courses, changing prerequisite requirements, revoking certifications |
| 15-60 duty days | Designated Master Instructor + C2DAO OIC written delegation | Full Training OIC authorities; delegation memorandum specifies scope and duration |
| > 60 duty days (vacancy) | Acting Training OIC designated by C2DAO Commander | Full Training OIC authorities; formal assumption of duties |

### 2-2. Minimum Bench Depth Targets

To ensure continuity, the training program maintains:

| Role | Minimum Target | Current Baseline |
|---|---|---|
| Master Instructor | 2 (minimum 1 at home station when OIC is TDY) | Establish NLT Q2 FY27 |
| Senior Instructor | 3 (across at least 2 course levels each) | Establish NLT Q1 FY27 |
| Certified Instructor | 1 per course offered per quarter | Current state per Instructor Roster |
| Unit Data Trainer | 1 per MSC (minimum) | Establish NLT Q4 FY26 aligned with MTT rotation |

### 2-3. Training OIC Transition Checklist

When a Training OIC transition occurs (PCS, reassignment, retirement):

**T-90 days (or upon notification of departure):**
- [ ] Identify successor candidate (Master Instructor preferred; Senior Instructor if no MI available)
- [ ] Begin knowledge transfer: weekly 1-hour sessions covering program administration, ongoing initiatives, external relationships
- [ ] Review and update Instructor Roster for accuracy
- [ ] Ensure all pending certifications, observations, and SME reviews are completed or formally transferred

**T-60 days:**
- [ ] Successor shadows Training OIC for at least 2 certification events (Phase 3 observation + Phase 4 certification)
- [ ] Successor receives access to all training program administrative systems
- [ ] Brief C2DAO Commander on transition status

**T-30 days:**
- [ ] Successor conducts at least 1 certification event independently (Training OIC observes)
- [ ] Draft transition memorandum documenting: open actions, pending reviews, upcoming course iterations, MTT schedule, known issues
- [ ] Update Instructor Roster with successor designation

**T-0 (departure):**
- [ ] Signed transition memorandum on file
- [ ] Commander signs designation of new Training OIC (or Acting Training OIC)
- [ ] Updated designation entered in Instructor Roster

## SECTION 3 — INSTRUCTOR BENCH DEVELOPMENT

### 3-1. Cross-Certification Strategy

The training program's resilience depends on instructor depth across courses. The cross-certification strategy ensures no course depends on a single instructor.

**Priority order for cross-certification:**
1. TM-10 and TM-20 — highest volume, most frequent delivery; every instructor should be certified for at least one of these
2. TM-30 — gateway to all TM-40 tracks; at least 2 certified instructors at all times
3. TM-40 WFF tracks (A-F) — cross-certify by adjacent WFF where domain expertise permits
4. TM-40/TM-50 specialist tracks (G-M) — cross-certification limited by domain expertise; prioritize the most frequently delivered tracks

**Annual cross-certification target:** Each instructor adds at least 1 cross-certification per year until certified for 3+ courses.

### 3-2. Senior Instructor Pipeline

The Training OIC maintains a Senior Instructor development pipeline:

1. Identify instructor candidates with 6+ months certification and 2+ iterations delivered
2. Assign mentoring responsibilities (supervise at least 1 Phase 2 candidate)
3. Prioritize observation of these candidates during annual observations
4. At 12 months + 4 iterations, initiate Senior Instructor recommendation review

Target: at least 1 new Senior Instructor candidate in the pipeline at all times.

### 3-3. Master Instructor Pipeline

The Training OIC identifies Master Instructor candidates from the Senior Instructor pool:

1. Senior Instructors with 18+ months in grade and 2+ cross-certifications enter consideration
2. Assign curriculum development responsibility (author or substantially revise 1 lesson plan)
3. Assign mentoring load (mentor at least 2 instructor candidates)
4. At 24 months as Senior, initiate Master Instructor designation review

Target: at least 1 Master Instructor candidate in the pipeline at all times.

## SECTION 4 — UNIT DATA TRAINER SUCCESSION

### 4-1. UDT Turnover Planning

Military units experience frequent personnel turnover (PCS, ETS, reassignment). UDT succession must account for this:

| Trigger | Action | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| UDT receives PCS orders | Unit commander nominates replacement for next T3-F iteration | NLT 60 days before departure |
| UDT changes duty position | Unit commander assesses whether new position retains training responsibilities; if not, nominates replacement | Within 30 days |
| UDT designation lapses | Unit commander nominates replacement or requests reinstatement | Within 30 days |
| New MSC established or reorganized | MSC commander nominates UDT candidate | Aligned with next MTT visit |

### 4-2. Overlap Period

When possible, the outgoing UDT and incoming UDT candidate should overlap for at least 1 TM-10 iteration:
- Incoming candidate observes outgoing UDT deliver TM-10
- Outgoing UDT provides local knowledge transfer (classroom setup, access procedures, unit-specific scheduling)
- This overlap does not replace T3-F certification — the incoming candidate must still complete T3-F

## SECTION 5 — ANNUAL SUCCESSION REVIEW

### 5-1. Review Process

The C2DAO Training OIC conducts an annual succession review (recommended: October, aligned with second PD session):

1. Review Instructor Roster for bench depth against targets in §2-2
2. Identify single-instructor courses (courses with only 1 certified instructor)
3. Review Senior and Master Instructor pipelines
4. Review UDT coverage by MSC
5. Identify risk areas and develop mitigation plan
6. Brief C2DAO Commander on succession posture

### 5-2. Succession Posture Report

The annual review produces a brief succession posture summary:

| Area | Green | Amber | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| Training OIC succession | Master Instructor designated; transition plan current | Senior Instructor identified; no Master Instructor | No identified successor |
| Instructor bench depth | All courses covered by 2+ instructors | 1-2 courses single-instructor | 3+ courses single-instructor |
| Senior Instructor pipeline | 1+ candidates meeting timeline | Candidates identified but not on timeline | No candidates |
| Master Instructor pipeline | 1+ candidates meeting timeline | Candidates identified but not on timeline | No candidates |
| UDT coverage | 1+ UDT per MSC | 50-99% MSC coverage | < 50% MSC coverage |
