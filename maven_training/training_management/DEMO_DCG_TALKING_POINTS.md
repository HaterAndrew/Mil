## ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS & RESPONSES

### "Why can't units just do this themselves?"
TM-10 is designed for unit-level execution — 8 hours, in-processing integration, no special instructor needed. That's already in the plan. But TM-20 and TM-30 require qualified instructors who understand Ontology architecture, Pipeline Builder, governance standards, and AIP Logic. No unit has those people organic to their formation. An MTT is the only way to deliver TM-20/30 at standard across the theater.

### "What does the MTT actually need — people, money, what?"
5 personnel: 1 OIC (O-3/CW3) and 4 instructors, organized as two teams (Alpha/Bravo). TDY funding for theater rotations across 5 locations. G1 identifies billets via TDA augmentation, G8 identifies TDY funding — both at D+30. MTT certifies under T3 standards by D+75, shadow-teaches with ODT D+90–D+150, and executes independently at D+150.

### "Is this duplicating anything TRADOC already does?"
No. TRADOC does not have an MSS-specific training program. Palantir offers product training but it doesn't cover data literacy, operational integration, or Army governance. No one else in the Army has built this. If it works, TRADOC can absorb it into the institutional domain — the governance package is already TRADOC-formatted for exactly that reason.

### "What about partner nations?"
Partner nations operating in the USAREUR-AF MSS environment have requested training access. The entire curriculum is UNCLASSIFIED and already integrates NATO data standards. Partner nation slots can be integrated into existing MTT rotations. G5 coordinates the enrollment framework by D+90. SJA reviews platform licensing and SOFA implications by D+30. COA 1 is the only option that delivers partner training.

### "What's the risk if we don't do this?"
Units continue ad-hoc self-training with inconsistent quality. MSS remains underutilized relative to its capability. ODT continues burning out on training instead of building. Partner nation demand goes unmet. And the Army's first opportunity to build an institutional MSS training capability dies on the vine while no one else is building one.

### "Who's teaching this? Are the instructors qualified?"
ODT personnel currently. Faculty Development Plan defines instructor qualification criteria. T3 (Instructor Certification) track is in development — targeted for completion at D+60. MTT cadre certifies under T3 by D+75, then shadow-teaches with ODT from D+90 to D+150 before operating independently. BSP graduates from TM-30 are also candidates for assistant roles.

### "Can this run without ODT?"
That's the end state. The governance package, self-study materials, and instructor certification track are designed so units can eventually execute organically. The MTT is the bridge — trains the first generation of unit-level instructors who take over. But ODT must be protected during standup: they maintain curriculum, train the MTT, and keep building. The TASKORD addresses ODT sustainment to prevent burnout.

### "What about Palantir IP? Are we allowed to build training on their product?"
SJA reviews this at D+30. The curriculum is entirely Army-produced. It teaches platform skills and data literacy — no Palantir proprietary code or documentation is reproduced. Similar to how the Army builds training on any fielded system. SJA confirms no IP conflict before the CG brief.

### "What's the timeline to full theater coverage?"
Phased approach. Phase 1 (D+0–D+90): sourcing, T3 certification, curriculum handoff. Phase 2 (D+90–D+150): MTT shadow-teaches alongside ODT. Phase 3 (D+150+): independent execution. With a 5-person MTT running the dual-team model, first full MSC rotation completes within 12 months of independent execution. Specialist tracks (TM-40/50) are self-study capable.

### "Could TRADOC pick this up?"
That's the long-term vision for Army-wide scaling. USAREUR-AF proves the model, TRADOC absorbs it. The governance package is already TRADOC-formatted. The curriculum is exportable to other ACOMs/ASCCs. But someone has to prove it works first — that's what COA 1 does.

### "How is this different from G4's DIU?"
Different scope, different problem. DIU is Palantir product training — it teaches staff how to navigate Vantage, use Contour, and operate Foundry tools. That's platform familiarization. MSS training starts where DIU stops. We teach data literacy, ontology architecture, pipeline engineering, governance standards, and operational integration across warfighting functions. DIU has ~7 courses focused on button-clicking; we have 22 published tracks covering specialists from ORSA to AI Engineer to Software Engineer, each with pre/post exams, hands-on exercises, and self-study paths. DIU also has no instructor certification, no faculty development plan, no TRADOC-formatted governance, and no scalability model — it runs when G4 schedules it and stops when they don't. Our program is designed for institutional adoption: MTT delivery, partner nation integration, and eventual TRADOC absorption. They're complementary — a Soldier who completes DIU's Foundation Operator Course is better prepared for our TM-10. But DIU does not and cannot replace what we built.

### "Is any of this classified?"
No. All curriculum is UNCLASSIFIED. The training hub is currently on Cloudflare Pages, migrating to a network subdomain. Course content teaches platform skills and data literacy — no operational data is included in training materials. This is why partner nation training is immediately executable with no releasability review required.
