## ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS & RESPONSES

### "Why 7ATC and not ODT?"
7ATC is the theater's institutional training command — this is what they do. ODT built the program, but ODT's core mission is building and maintaining MSS capability, not running a training pipeline. If ODT runs training, it competes directly with their build mission. 7ATC gives it an institutional home. ODT stays as proponent — we maintain the curriculum, certify instructors, and update content. Best of both.

### "What if ODT just keeps doing it?"
That's COA 2. It works if we grow the team. Right now every class ODT teaches is time off their core mission. If the command resources dedicated training billets within ODT, it's viable. But it means ODT is no longer purely a build team — it becomes a build-and-train team. That's a deliberate organizational decision.

### "Why not G37?"
G37 has the training authorities but not the technical depth. MSS training requires instructors who understand ontology architecture, pipeline engineering, and AIP Logic. G37 would need significant ramp-up time and would still depend on ODT for curriculum and instructor certification. It's possible but it's the longest standup.

### "Why can't units just do this themselves?"
SL 1 is designed for unit-level execution — 8 hours, in-processing integration, no special instructor needed. That's already in the plan. But SL 2 and SL 3 require qualified instructors who understand ontology architecture, Pipeline Builder, governance standards, and AIP Logic. No unit has those people organic to their formation.

### "Is this duplicating anything TRADOC already does?"
No. TRADOC does not have an MSS-specific training program. Palantir offers product training but it doesn't cover data literacy, operational integration, or Army governance. No one else in the Army has built this. If it works, TRADOC can absorb it — the governance package is already TRADOC-formatted for exactly that reason.

### "What about partner nations?"
Partner nations operating in the USAREUR-AF MSS environment have requested training access. The entire curriculum already integrates NATO data standards. Partner nation slots integrate into existing training rotations. G5 coordinates the enrollment framework. SJA reviews platform licensing and SOFA implications.

### "What's the risk if we don't do this?"
Units continue ad-hoc self-training with inconsistent quality. MSS remains underutilized relative to its capability. ODT continues burning out on training instead of building. The Army's first opportunity to build an institutional MSS training capability stops advancing while no one else is building one.

### "What about Palantir IP? Are we allowed to build training on their product?"
The curriculum is entirely Army-produced. It teaches platform skills and data literacy — no Palantir proprietary code or documentation is reproduced. Similar to how the Army builds training on any fielded system. SJA confirms no IP conflict.

### "Could TRADOC pick this up?"
That's the long-term vision for Army-wide scaling. USAREUR-AF proves the model, TRADOC absorbs it. The governance package is already TRADOC-formatted. The curriculum is exportable to other ACOMs/ASCCs. But someone has to prove it works first.

### "How is this different from G4's DIU?"
Different problem, different depth, different outcome.

**DIU** is platform familiarization — how to navigate the interface, click through menus, run pre-built searches. ~7 courses, no assessment, no certification, no governance alignment. It answers the question: *"How do I use the tool?"*

**CDA Training** answers the harder question: *"How do I build with the tool and make it operationally relevant?"* Data literacy, ontology architecture, pipeline engineering, governance standards, and integration across warfighting functions. 272 published PDFs across 6 course levels, each with pre/post exams, hands-on exercises, and self-study tracks. TRADOC-formatted governance package. Instructor certification pipeline. Coalition-ready from day one.

| | DIU | CDA Training |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Platform navigation | Data literacy → pipeline engineering |
| Courses | ~7 | 6 levels, 22+ tracks |
| Assessment | None | Pre/post exams every level |
| Hands-on exercises | No | Yes, every track |
| Governance alignment | No | TRADOC-formatted |
| Instructor certification | No | Yes |
| Coalition-ready | No | Yes — NATO standards |
| Progression model | Flat | SL 1 → SL 5, feeds project pipeline |

They're complementary, not competing. A Soldier who completes DIU is better prepared for SL 1. But DIU produces users — CDA training produces builders. That's the gap no one else is filling.

### "Is any of this classified?"
No. Course content teaches platform skills and data literacy — no operational data is included. This is why partner nation training is immediately executable with no releasability review required.
