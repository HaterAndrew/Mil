# Unified Data Transition Strategy — Quarterly Product Cycle

**Classification:** CUI
**Authority:** USAREUR-AF Chief Technology Officer (CTO) / Operational Data Team (ODT)

---

## Overview

The Unified Data Transition Strategy defines the quarterly product cycle for identifying, developing, and deploying data capabilities across USAREUR-AF. The cycle moves through two phases — **Discovery & Framing** and **Iteration & Implementation** — with a CG-chaired Priority Steering Board (PSB) as the forcing function between phases.

---

## Phase 1: Discovery & Framing

### Problem Identification
- Identifying gaps or opportunities to integrate data to enhance combat capabilities
- Inputs from Domain SMEs (Warfighting Function leads) and Project Owners

### Foundry Bootcamp
- Upskilling and professional development
- Focused tech-stack research ("spiking")
- Defining "left and right limits" of capability
- Participants: Bootcamp attendees from across the command

### Capability Awareness Days (CADs)
- Exposure to on-contract capabilities
- Bridge between talent-development and prioritization

---

## Priority Steering Board (PSB) — CG Chaired Event

- Product submissions are evaluated
- Assess and allocate resources
- Product submissions accepted continuously; submissions occurring after this board are eligible for the next cycle

### Resource Allocation & Distribution of Work

Resources are drawn from multiple channels:
- **G6** — Signal/IT infrastructure support
- **Vendors** — On-contract capability providers
- **ODT** — Operational Data Team organic capacity
- **New Hire / Procurement** — Expansion capacity
- **Partners** — External collaboration
- **Directorate Internal** — Staff directorate organic resources

---

## Phase 2: Iteration & Implementation

### Proof of Concept (PoC)
- Software/product engineering
- Product design and ongoing user testing

### Forcing Function (e.g., Exercise)
- Tests and evaluates new capability in operational context
- Determine course of action: **persevere**, **pivot**, or **divest**

---

## Working Spheres

### External Working Sphere
- **Domain SMEs (WfF)** — Warfighting function subject matter experts who define requirements
- **Project Owners** — Sponsors who own the operational problem
- **Bootcamp Participants** — Personnel undergoing upskilling

### CTO Sphere of Responsibility
- **Palantir Support** — Platform vendor technical support
- **Data SMEs (ODT)** — Operational Data Team data engineers and analysts
- **Product Managers (ODT)** — Product ownership and backlog management
- **Facilitation & Stewardship (ODT)** — Process governance and data stewardship
- **Data Integration (G6)** — Network and systems integration

---

## Relationship to Training

This product cycle directly informs the training pipeline:
- **Problem Identification** feeds TM-40A–F (WFF tracks) requirements
- **Foundry Bootcamp** aligns with TM-20/TM-30 curriculum
- **CADs** support TM-10 awareness objectives
- **PoC development** drives TM-40G–O (specialist track) skill requirements
- **Exercise validation** generates lessons learned for curriculum updates
