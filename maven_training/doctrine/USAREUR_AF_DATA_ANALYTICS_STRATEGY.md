# USAREUR-AF Data and Analytics Strategy


**Published:** May 2025
**Authority:** USAREUR-AF G3 Command Chief Data and Analytics Officer (C2DAO)
**Signed by:** GEN Christopher T. Donahue, Commanding General, USAREUR-AF

---

## 1. Introduction

The *U.S. Army Europe and Africa Data and Analytics Strategy* outlines the vision and desired outcomes in the data and analytics space for USAREUR-AF over the next three to five years. This strategy is aligned with Department of Defense (DoD) and Headquarters, Department of the Army (HQDA) data and digital modernization guidance.

The DoD and HQDA have long recognized data as a foundational strategic asset required to drive decision making and optimize operations. As stated in the HQDA Army Data Plan, "Data enabled decisions, decisions that will outpace an adversary, will decide future battles."

### 1.1 Target Audience and Purpose

This strategy is designed for USAREUR-AF, its Major Subordinate Commands (MSCs), and assigned units. Its purpose is to define the vision and future state for a data-centric theater enterprise organization.

This strategy outlines USAREUR-AF's approach to data-centricity to the broader community both within and outside USAREUR-AF's area of responsibility (AOR), including USEUCOM, USAFRICOM, USAFE-AFAFRICA, NAVEUR-NAVAF, USARCENT, and USARPAC.

### 1.2 Background

The DoD and HQDA based their current direction in data and analytics on the Evidence-Based Policymaking Act of 2018, which set Federal goals for data sharing and management.

Multiple organizations across the DoD — including DoD Chief Digital and Artificial Intelligence Office (CDAO), Chief Information Officer (CIO), HQDA, and USEUCOM — have recognized the need for an enterprise approach to current data capability gaps. In 2023, the Mission Command Center of Excellence (MCCoE) summarized current data-centric challenges in their *Decision-Driven Data Concept of Operations* (6 July 2023).

Treating data as an enterprise resource means managing and governing data like any other asset. The shift toward data-centricity will have a broad-reaching impact on USAREUR-AF's people, processes, and technologies.

## 2. Vision for Data-Driven Operations

USAREUR-AF is committed to becoming a fully data-centric organization, utilizing data to the maximum extent to achieve decision dominance.

> **Vision:** Leverage data at speed and scale for decision dominance and optimized operations.

**Decision dominance** is "a desired state in which commanders sense, understand, decide, act, and assess faster and more effectively than their adversaries" (CSA Paper #1, 16 March 2021). Decision dominance requires situational understanding to make fast and effective decisions — also known as **decision advantage**.

To drive decision advantage at the strategic, operational, and tactical levels, USAREUR-AF will promote data as an enterprise resource and leverage advanced analytics and artificial intelligence (AI) across the theater. As the modern data ecosystem evolves, data becomes a force multiplier.

### 2.1 Data: A Force Multiplier — The Cognitive Hierarchy

The Cognitive Hierarchy (expanding on JP 3-0 and ADP 3-13) illustrates how data enables decision advantage:

```
Decision Advantage  ← Experience & Leadership
  ↑
Shared Understanding ← Judgement & Collaboration
  ↑
Knowledge            ← Sharing knowledge to create shared understanding
  ↑
Information          ← Systems & Analysis
  ↑
Data                 ← Managing information
```

1. **Data** → processed and analyzed → generates **Information**
2. **Information** → application of judgement → becomes **Knowledge**
3. **Knowledge** → shared through collaboration → creates **Shared Understanding**
4. **Shared Understanding** → viewed with experience and leadership → translates into **Decision Advantage**

As decisions are turned into actions, additional data is collected in a continuous feedback loop which then informs future decisions.

### 2.2 VAULTIS Data Attributes

Seven core attributes enhance the quality of data used in the Cognitive Hierarchy, identified in the Army Data Plan (October 2022):

| Attribute | Definition |
|---|---|
| **V**isible | Consumers can locate the needed data |
| **A**ccessible | Consumers can retrieve the data |
| **U**nderstandable | Consumers can find descriptions of data to recognize the content, context, and applicability |
| **L**inked | Consumers can exploit complementary data elements through innate relationships |
| **T**rustworthy | Consumers can be confident in all aspects of data for decision-making |
| **I**nteroperable | Consumers and producers have a common representation and comprehension of data |
| **S**ecure | Consumers know that data is protected from unauthorized use and manipulation |

> "Army and Joint leaders have referred to data as the new ammunition. [...] Like ammunition, data does not appear on the battlefield without management." — Army Data Plan (October 2022)

## 3. Strategic Outcomes and Objectives

Four strategic outcomes enable data-centricity to achieve decision dominance. These outcomes focus on handling data as a "strategic asset that must be operationalized" (DoD Data Strategy, 2020).

### Outcome 1: Decision Advantage

Decision advantage requires both an understanding of the Commander's intent and the required live data to appropriately inform decisions. Data must be presented in a consumable way so that the commander can quickly assess the current situation and execute efficient, effective data-driven operations.

- **Objective 1.1:** Identified and prioritized live data needs
- **Objective 1.2:** Enhanced data visualization capabilities
- **Objective 1.3:** Integrated analytics and course of action (COA) capabilities
- **Objective 1.4:** Efficient data-enabled business processes and workflows

### Outcome 2: Data Interoperability

Data interoperability is a critical enabler for operations with Joint Forces, NATO HQ, Allies, and partners. Defining and using trusted data sources, data access specifications, and data standards enable data interoperability. Information interoperability takes data a step further, providing semantic context to create knowledge and shared understanding from which decisions can be made.

- **Objective 2.1:** Identified trusted data sources and cataloged critical data and access interfaces
- **Objective 2.2:** Improved information sharing and interoperability with Joint Forces, NATO HQ, Allies, and partners
- **Objective 2.3:** Established data sharing agreements with Allies and partners

### Outcome 3: Modernize Theater Data Infrastructure

To achieve data-centricity, USAREUR-AF must provide the infrastructure required to enable access to accurate, timely, and trusted data as outlined in the Army's Unified Data Reference Architecture (UDRA). This requires implementing a modern data ecosystem to facilitate interoperability and improve legacy data exchange capabilities.

- **Objective 3.1:** Theater data architecture, leveraging open standards, vendor agnostic capabilities, and zero trust data security measures
- **Objective 3.2:** Streamlined cross-domain solutions
- **Objective 3.3:** Infrastructure capabilities to support data production, consumption, and orchestration
- **Objective 3.4:** Alignment with Army, combatant command (CCMD), and DoD policy and guidance

### Outcome 4: Data-Ready Workforce and Processes

The workforce (leaders, service members, Civilians, and contractors) is recognized as a critical enabler to the success of any effort. USAREUR-AF must prioritize the development of a data-literate workforce that understands and can articulate the data needs of each warfighting function.

- **Objective 4.1:** A sustained data and analytics organization and governance framework
- **Objective 4.2:** Partnerships with industry and academia
- **Objective 4.3:** Recruit, retain, and train the workforce
- **Objective 4.4:** Culture of innovation through experimentation

---

## 4. Collaboration

USAREUR-AF will continue to work with the MSCs and the Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD) CDAO, HQDA CDAO, its CCMDs — USEUCOM Chief Data Officer (CDO) and USAFRICOM CDO — as well as NATO HQ, Allies, partners, and other AOR service components. These engagements will focus on promoting interoperability and accelerating the realization of Combined Joint All Domain Command and Control (CJADC2), the foundational data integration layer, and the efficient use of data and artificial intelligence resources to avoid duplicative or competing efforts.

---

## 5. Conclusion and Next Steps

This strategy defines the vision for a future state data-centric theater organization. It will be followed by two supportive products:

1. **Data and Analytics Strategic Action Plan** — lines of effort (LOE) for achieving the outcomes and objectives, developed in collaboration with key stakeholders across the theater.
2. **Data and Analytics Governance Framework** — governance structure for data and analytics efforts at strategic, operational, and tactical levels according to Commander priorities and this strategy.

This is a living document that will be reviewed and updated as the intent, mission, and operational requirements continue to evolve.

## Appendix A: VAULTIS Assessment Questions

| Attribute | Assessment Questions |
|---|---|
| **Visible** | Are authorized users able to discover the existence of data? Are data stewards assigned responsibility for identifying, registering, and exposing data? Are users able to discover who is responsible for specific data products? |
| **Accessible** | Are authorized users able to obtain the data they need when they need it? Are warfighting, intelligence, and business data accessible to authorized users? |
| **Understandable** | Are tools in place to securely aggregate, compare, and analyze data? Are data labeled and formatted to support large scale analytics? Does metadata contain contextual relationships and business rules? |
| **Linked** | Are data linked such that relationships and dependencies can be uncovered? Does the organization use industry best practices for open data standards, data catalogs, and metadata tagging? |
| **Trustworthy** | Does the data represent a source of truth? Must the data undergo additional vetting? Can users interpret and analyze data without concern for flawed assumptions? |
| **Interoperable** | Can data be exchanged between systems and users while maintaining quality and usability? Do data have semantic as well as syntactic interoperability? |
| **Secure** | Is data protected at rest, in motion, and in use? Does the organization use attribute-based access control? Are classified management procedures in place? |

## Appendix C: Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Decision Advantage** | Acquiring timely, accurate, and relevant data and information to gain situational understanding; rapidly translating that understanding into quality decisions; and swiftly disseminating those decisions in a useable and shareable format (AFC Pam 71-20-9) |
| **Decision Dominance** | A desired state in which commanders sense, understand, decide, act, and assess faster and more effectively than their adversaries (CSA Paper #1) |
| **Data-Centric** | An environment where data is the primary and permanent asset separated from systems/applications, making data available to a broad range of tools and analytics within and across security domains (DoD Data Strategy) |
| **Data Product** | Logically pre-packaged data and associated metadata produced to satisfy consumers' mission or business demand (UDRA V 1.0) |
| **Data Architecture** | A set of rules, policies, standards, and models that govern and define the type of data collected and how it is used, stored, managed, and integrated |
| **Data Mesh** | A data architecture based on a federated and decentralized approach to analytical data production, management, and sharing (UDRA V 1.0) |
