Awesome ask. Here's a **practical field guide** to **ADP 3-13: *Information* (Nov 2023)** — distilled for architects, planners, and builders — with doctrinal anchors so your team can execute confidently.

# What ADP 3-13 Changes (in one breath)

**Information is treated as part of the operational environment and combat power, not a side specialty.** Every commander, staff section, and Soldier has a role in creating, protecting, exploiting, and attacking information to gain **information advantage** across MDO. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

---

# Core ideas you should adopt

### Information Advantage (IA)

**Aim:** Out-understand, out-decide, and out-pace the threat by integrating information into everything we do.
**Reality:** IA is **relative and temporary**—you must pursue it, defend it, and exploit it continuously. ([armyupress.army.mil][2])

### Five Information Activities

Use these as your universal verbs to plan and assess:

1. **Enable** (C2, sensing, data flow)
2. **Protect** (OPSEC, hardening, emission control)
3. **Inform** (facts to friendly/neutral audiences)
4. **Influence** (change perceptions/behaviors)
5. **Attack** (deny, degrade, deceive adversary info functions) ([DefenseScoop][3])

### Three OE Dimensions (your integration scaffold)

* **Physical** (reach/survive/sustain)
* **Information** (know/decide/deny/deceive)
* **Human** (will/legitimacy/cohesion)
  These interact continuously in the **information environment**. ([bits.de][4])

# Planning & Execution Playbook

### 1) Commander's Intent (add an "IA paragraph")

* **Desired IA effects:** what must be enabled, protected, informed, influenced, attacked.
* **Priority audiences/systems:** friendly C2 & partners; threat decision-makers, sensors, and C2.
* **Tempo & risk:** IA windows you must create/protect (before, during, after decisive action). ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

### 2) Information Environment Analysis (IEA)

Fold this into IPB/MDMP products:

* **Key nodes & pathways:** data sources, networks, spectrum, platforms, social channels.
* **Cognitive map:** priority audiences, beliefs, decision cycles, influence leaders.
* **Adversary info kill chain:** sense → understand → decide → act (where to disrupt).
* **Blue emissions & signatures:** what your ops "say" unintentionally. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

### 3) Course of Action (COA) Design with the Five Activities

For each COA, explicitly script **Enable/Protect/Inform/Influence/Attack** tasks by phase:

* **Enable:** data feeds, C2 pathways, cross-domain movement of required info.
* **Protect:** OPSEC measures, spectrum discipline, hardening, deception cover.
* **Inform:** time-phased truth to friendly/partner civil/military audiences (PAO alignment).
* **Influence:** target behaviors (support, compliance, non-interference); means & measures.
* **Attack:** targeting of adversary info functions (legal/authorities coordinated). ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

### 4) Targeting & Synchronization

* Put IA effects on the **running estimate**, **TSS/MAAP** equivalents, and **fires** timelines; many fires produce **protect/influence/attack** info effects—treat them deliberately. ([armyupress.army.mil][2])

### 5) Assessment (continuous)

* **MoEs:** audience understanding, decision latency deltas, behavior change, adversary ISR/C2 disruption.
* **MoPs:** message delivery, node hardening status, latency/jitter, emission compliance, deception discipline.
* **Re-task** activities as windows open/close. IA is perishable. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

# Roles & Integration (who does what)

* **Commander:** owns IA; sets priority audiences/nodes; accepts risk. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])
* **G-3/7 (or G-39/IO) cell:** integrates the five activities into operations; battle rhythm & targeting. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])
* **G-2:** IEA inside IPB; adversary info kill chain; audience analysis. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])
* **G-6/CEMA:** networks, spectrum, cyber hardening; enable/protect; offensive support to "attack." ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])
* **PAO / MISO / CA / MILDEC / OPSEC / Space / EW:** capabilities mapped to the five activities—plan as **combined arms for information**. ([The Lightning Press SMARTbooks][5])

# Builder's Checklist (for data & tooling teams)

**Enable**

* Mission-data catalog (sources, freshness, trust); **latency SLOs** for critical feeds.
* Common schema + lineage; red/blue/partner data access plans. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

**Protect**

* Emission control playbook; telemetry minimization; OPSEC scanning for dashboards & exports.
* Cyber hardening on data paths; deception covers for signatures. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

**Inform**

* Fact pipelines to commanders/partners (dashboards, briefs); measure reach & comprehension. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

**Influence**

* Audience segmentation in the data model; behavior-based KPIs; counter-narrative triggers. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

**Attack**

* Target system model: ISR sensors, comms, C2 nodes; "break the loop" points; cross-domain effects. ([armyupress.army.mil][2])

# Measures You Can Steal (right now)

**Speed:** median time from sensor → commander's decision (by vignette/phase).
**Coherence:** % of products aligned to commander's narrative/IA priorities.
**Signature:** change in friendly detectable emissions vs. baseline during critical windows.
**Friction to Threat:** increase in adversary decision latency or targeting error rate.
**Behavior:** observable audience/action change mapped to intended effect. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

# "Do/Don't" for Commanders & Staff

**Do**

* Write an **IA paragraph** in CDR's intent and task to purpose.
* Put **five activities** into every COA, by phase, with owners and measures.
* Treat **fires, movements, and sustainment** as informational acts—plan second-order effects. ([armyupress.army.mil][2])

**Don't**

* Silo "information" to a single shop.
* Flood the force with data—optimize for **decision speed** and **narrative coherence**.
* Ignore your own signatures: friendly info is an **outcome to manage**. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

# Ready-to-use task verbs (drop into orders)

* **Enable:** establish, federate, fuse, disseminate, assure, accelerate
* **Protect:** conceal, reduce, harden, obfuscate, sanitize, compartmentalize
* **Inform:** publish, brief, display, alert, report, expose
* **Influence:** prime, frame, reassure, deter, compel, delegitimize
* **Attack:** deny, degrade, deceive, corrupt, isolate, overload ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])

# What to train this month

1. **IEA drill:** draw your adversary info kill chain; pick two break points; war-game counters.
2. **Signature control lab:** run your own dashboards through OPSEC/sig checks; fix leaks.
3. **Narrative rehearsal:** 3-slide "truth to force/partners" product, aligned to CDR's IA effects.
4. **Assessment reps:** pick two MoEs + three MoPs; update weekly in the running estimate. ([rdl.train.army.mil][1])
