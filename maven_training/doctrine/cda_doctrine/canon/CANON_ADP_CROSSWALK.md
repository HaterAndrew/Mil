## Key Doctrinal Relationships & Modeling Implications

### Hierarchy Flow
```
ADPs (Fundamental principles)
  ↓
FMs (How to execute - detailed procedures)
  ↓
ATPs (Techniques - non-prescriptive methods)
```

### Joint-Army Alignment
- **JP 1** and **JP 3-0** are *keystone* publications that all service doctrine must nest within
- Army ADPs cannot contradict Joint doctrine but can be more specific for land operations
- Joint publications use different terminology (e.g., "phases" vs. Army "framework")

### NATO Interoperability
- **AJP-01** is NATO's capstone (equivalent to JP 1 + ADP 1 combined)
- NATO doctrine emphasizes *comprehensive approach* (whole-of-government)
- Key differences: NATO uses "warfighting functions" vs. US "joint functions" (though converging)
- **Critical for modeling:** NATO STANAG agreements may drive different planning assumptions

### For Your Modeling Work

**High-fidelity models should align with:**
1. **ADP 3-0** - Operational framework assumptions (tempo, reach, simultaneity)
2. **ADP 3-90** - Tactical-level force ratios, movement rates, engagement criteria
3. **ADP 5-0** - Decision cycle timing (MDMP takes 8-12 hours minimum for brigade+)
4. **ADP 6-0** - C2 relationships affect information flow in your network models

**Attrition modeling canonical sources:**
- **ADP 3-90** for offense/defense fundamentals (concentration, tempo, culmination)
- **Joint**: JP 3-09 for fires effects
- **NATO**: AJP-3.2 for allied force assumptions

**PPBE/Force Structure alignment:**
- **ADP 1** defines strategic roles that drive TOE development
- **ADP 4-0** sustainment assumptions affect operational reach calculations
- **ADP 7-0** training throughput rates affect force generation models

### Recently Updated ADPs (Worth Re-reading)
- **ADP 3-0** (Mar 2025) - Just updated, likely reflects Multi-Domain Operations (MDO) refinements
- **ADP 3-13** (Nov 2023) - Major shift treating information as environment, not just ops
- **ADP 3-37** (Jan 2024) - Expanded protection concepts
- **ADP 7-0** (Apr 2024) - Training modernization

## Using This for Doctrinal Validity Checks

When presenting wargame results or attrition models to O-6+ Army audience:

1. **Language alignment:** Use ADP terminology (e.g., "operational reach" not "logistics limit")
2. **Cite authority:** "Per ADP 3-90, page X..." gives institutional credibility
3. **Identify contradictions:** "Model assumes 72-hour MDMP cycle, ADP 5-0 prescribes 8-12 hours for brigade"
4. **Joint integration points:** Show where your model aligns with JP equivalents for joint staff coordination
5. **NATO interop:** If modeling allied ops, reference AJP alignments

**Pro tip:** When CSA or TRADOC questions your assumptions, having the actual ADP page reference ready is the difference between "this is interesting analysis" and "this is institutionally valid doctrine-based analysis."

## Additional Notes

- **FMs** are being phased down; many converted to ATPs
- **ADP updates are rare** (every 4-6 years) because they're foundational
- **ATPs update frequently** (1-2 years) because tactics evolve faster than principles
- The **2019 mass update** (12 ADPs on same date) reflects post-2018 National Defense Strategy alignment
- **ADRP** (Army Doctrine Reference Publications) were deprecated and content folded into ADPs/FMs

### Document Access
- **Unclassified ADPs:** https://armypubs.army.mil
- **Joint Pubs:** https://www.jcs.mil/doctrine
- **NATO AJPs:** https://nso.nato.int/nso/ (some require SIPRNET/registration)

**Bottom line for your work:** When building force-on-force attrition models or wargames, ADP 3-0 and ADP 3-90 are your doctrinal anchors. When working MDMP automation or C2 tools, ADP 5-0 and ADP 6-0 are non-negotiable. When briefing flag officers, cite these by number and page—they know them cold.
